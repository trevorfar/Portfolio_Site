Hey, I'm Trevor Farias, an aspiring software / hardware engineer, originally from Salt Spring Island, BC. I am currently a second-year Computer Engineering student at Queens University in Ontario, but my passion for everything technology started when I got my first shared-2DS in 2013. Thanks grandma! Ever since, anything and everything digital has been my lifeline.

As a kid, video games were my creative outlet, leading me to establish and manage Minecraft servers. I created custom plugins using Java and C, while also delving into network management, using tools like Wireshark and Burp Suite. I was the sole developer, while also maintaining a player-base, which turned out to be a lot harder than I could have imagined. Through this experience, I learned how technology fuses computers with the human experience and I was challenged with the difficult balance of making sure my community was happy, while also creating innovative and new updates.

Recently, my focus has shifted toward cybersecurity. I've been exploring exciting opportunities in platforms, such as Hackthebox and Hackthissite. Creating a Linux clone on VMHardware has become a pivotal aspect of my experimentation, serving as the foundation for most of my software endeavors.
